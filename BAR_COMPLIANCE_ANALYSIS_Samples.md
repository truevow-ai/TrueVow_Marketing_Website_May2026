### **CATEGORY 1: STRUCTURAL & FRAMING IMPROVEMENTS (8)**

#### 1. **Synthesis Approach**
- **What:** Explicit statement that the guide "synthesizes obligations set forth in the MSA, Terms, and Privacy Policy"
- **Why:** Establishes the compliance guide as the **unified reference** for all contractual obligations
- **Location:** Opening paragraph
- **Value:** HIGH — provides legal coherence and cross-document integration

#### 2. **Central Principle Statement**
- **What:** "The central principle of the TrueVow platform is that the Customer retains full and final professional and ethical responsibility for all outputs, client interactions, and regulatory compliance."
- **Why:** Single sentence that distills the entire liability allocation
- **Location:** Opening paragraph
- **Value:** CRITICAL — this is the "north star" of the entire compliance framework

#### 3. **Cross-References to Terms/MSA Sections**
- **What:** Throughout the document, specific citations to "Terms Section 4.2," "MSA 3.4," "Terms 5.1," etc.
- **Why:** Allows attorneys to verify each compliance statement against the binding contract
- **Location:** Throughout
- **Value:** HIGH — increases legal defensibility and attorney trust

#### 4. **"Core Logic Covenant" Concept**
- **What:** "The Firm acknowledges that TrueVow's Core Logic is deterministic and rule-based (Terms 5.1). This deterministic nature is intended to support the Firm's compliance by avoiding the use of generative AI in legal qualification, but **does not transfer any ethical responsibility** for the resulting output."
- **Why:** Explicitly disclaims that deterministic = no attorney responsibility
- **Location:** Section 1.1
- **Value:** CRITICAL — prevents attorneys from claiming "the algorithm made the decision"

#### 5. **"Segregation of Duty" Concept**
- **What:** "The Firm is assured that Ancillary ML Tools **do not influence, participate in, or affect any legal or quasi-legal determination** of the Core Logic"
- **Why:** Separates operational ML (transcription) from legal logic (qualification)
- **Location:** Section 2.1
- **Value:** HIGH — defends against claims that "AI made legal decisions"

#### 6. **"Final Liability Transfer" Section Title**
- **What:** Renaming indemnification section to "Final Liability Transfer"
- **Why:** More direct and business-focused language
- **Location:** Section 5
- **Value:** MEDIUM — clearer communication of risk allocation

#### 7. **Module-Specific Compliance Sections**
- **What:** Dedicated subsections for DRAFT, SETTLE, CONNECT, VERIFY (not just INTAKE)
- **Why:** Each service has unique ethical risks that must be addressed separately
- **Location:** Sections 1.2, 2.2, 2.3, 4.1
- **Value:** CRITICAL — without this, the guide is incomplete

#### 8. **"Duty of Technology Competence" Framing (Rule 1.1)**
- **What:** Reframing all technology discussions under ABA Model Rule 1.1 (Competence)
- **Why:** Ties TrueVow usage to the attorney's affirmative duty to understand technology
- **Location:** Section 2 title
- **Value:** HIGH — shifts burden to attorney to be competent in tech use
