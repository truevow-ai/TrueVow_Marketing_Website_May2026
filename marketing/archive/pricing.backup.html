Frequently Asked Questions

Pricing & Billing
How does the pricing model work?
TrueVow has three public plans. SOLO is a simple subscription at $499/month with all intake records visible and no per-lead unlock fees. GROWTH is $1,499/month and includes 21 A+ lead unlocks. TEAM is $2,999/month and includes 55 A+ lead unlocks.

All plans include usage limits based on routed intake calls or voice minutes, whichever comes first. All plans require approval, onboarding configuration, and a successful test call before activation.
What is a qualified lead unlock?
An A+ lead unlock applies to a high-priority opportunity flagged by Benjamin under your firm-approved intake criteria. Growth and Team plans include monthly A+ unlocks. Additional A+ unlocks are billed only after included unlocks are used.

SOLO does not use per-lead unlock billing. All Solo intake records remain visible, with up to 5 A+ priority opportunities included before an upgrade review may be recommended.

Benjamin flags intake priority for attorney review. TrueVow does not determine legal merit.
Can I upgrade or downgrade my plan?
Plan changes can be made through the customer portal after approval. Start with SOLO and upgrade as your volume grows. Availability may depend on onboarding capacity.
Is Spanish really included?
Yes. Spanish intake support is included. During onboarding, the firm confirms when Spanish intake should be offered and how Spanish-language callers should be routed, booked, or escalated.
How It Works
Is Benjamin a chatbot?
No. Benjamin is not a free-form chatbot. Benjamin follows firm-approved intake paths, routing rules, escalation logic, and recording settings. It can support yes/no questions, structured responses, and approved open-ended prompts, but it is not designed to provide legal advice or make attorney judgment calls.
What happens when a call comes in?
When Benjamin is enabled for a routed call, it follows the firm's approved greeting, screening questions, escalation rules, booking rules, and recording/transcript settings. Benjamin creates a structured intake record and alerts the firm according to the plan and configuration.
What's the onboarding process?
1. Apply — Submit firm details
2. Review — Application and county availability reviewed
3. Onboard — Intake path, calendar, routing, and recording settings configured
4. Go Live — Test call completed before activation

Onboarding is limited to maintain quality and personal attention.
What practice areas does TrueVow support?
TrueVow is currently positioned for solo and small plaintiff-side PI firms. Additional practice areas may be reviewed during onboarding, but the public product is optimized around PI intake, qualification, and case screening.
Compliance & Security
What about compliance and data handling?
TrueVow is designed for controlled data handling, not zero-knowledge storage. Depending on firm configuration, the system may process contact details, structured intake answers, lead grades, booking details, risk flags, optional recordings, and optional transcripts.

Recording and transcript generation are optional and controlled by firm policy. Case analysis workflows do not require client identifiers where they are not needed. TrueVow is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice.
Are recordings and transcripts included?
Recording and transcript features are optional and configured during onboarding based on firm policy, disclosure requirements, and jurisdiction. Solo includes short-term retention if enabled. Growth and Team may support extended retention options depending on plan and approval.
Who Is This For?
Do you serve large firms or enterprise?
TrueVow is built for solo and small PI firms. Larger firms or unusual workflows may be reviewed separately, but the public product is designed for small firm intake, booking, and lead-review workflows.
Why publish pricing instead of requiring a demo?
TrueVow is built for solo and small PI firms, not enterprise procurement committees. We publish starting prices so firm owners can understand the basic economics before applying. Final activation still happens after approval inside the customer portal because setup, county availability, recording policy, calendar configuration, and product fit must be reviewed first.
