What TRACE Tracks

The operational pieces that block demand preparation — kept current and visible.

Providers

Treating providers identified, confirmed, and organized per matter.

Records

Record requests and receipt status tracked to completion.

Bills

Itemized bills received vs. outstanding, by provider.

Liens

Lien identification and status visibility for the file.

Treatment gaps

Gaps in the treatment timeline flagged for firm review.

Missing items

Outstanding items that still block case readiness.

Chronology drafts

Source-linked chronology drafts prepared for attorney review.

Review status

Attorney and paralegal review state shown at a glance.

What TRACE Does Not Do

TRACE is case-readiness operations support. It organizes factual items for attorney review. It does not interpret medicine, value cases, or recommend settlement or legal decisions.

No legal advice
No medical advice
No causation opinions
No injury-severity opinions
No settlement recommendations
No case valuation

Attorney review is required. TRACE does not provide legal advice, medical advice, causation opinions, injury-severity opinions, case valuation, or settlement recommendations. The firm reviews all outputs and decides what happens next.
