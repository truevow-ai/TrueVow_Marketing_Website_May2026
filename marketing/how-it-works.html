Stage 1 — INTAKE

Benjamin Answers and Qualifies

When a potential client calls your firm, calls can route to Benjamin based on your configured schedule, routing rules, and intake setup. Benjamin answers routed calls when enabled, helping reduce voicemail leakage and missed opportunities. Benjamin walks through a structured, practice-specific intake path using pre-approved question branches configured for the firm. The flow can support yes/no questions, short structured responses, and open-ended caller responses where the firm has approved them.

How Benjamin handles the call

  • Answers routed calls when enabled, based on the firm's configured routing schedule
  • Follows deterministic intake controls with pre-approved question paths configured for the firm
  • Can support yes/no questions, structured responses, and approved open-ended prompts
  • Captures caller responses into structured intake records with key facts, scores, and risk flags
  • Audio recording and transcript generation are optional and controlled by the law firm's retention policy

Why deterministic, not generative AI

Benjamin is designed to operate with deterministic intake controls and minimal generative AI. The firm controls the approved question paths, escalation rules, and recording policy. Where open-ended responses are captured, the system records and structures the caller's answer without giving legal advice or making legal judgments.

What this means for compliance

You review and approve the intake script before it goes live. Benjamin is designed to follow your approved rules and escalation paths. If a caller says something that triggers a risk flag (e.g., timing concern, adverse party admission), Benjamin routes accordingly and flags it for your review.

Lead Grading: A+ / B / C / D

After each call, Benjamin assigns a lead grade based on qualification criteria you define. This is not guesswork — it is a deterministic score based on the answers provided.

Booking + Priority Alert

Qualified leads can be booked into your calendar based on your firm’s configured rules. You receive a priority SMS alert with the key details: who called, why they called, and when the consultation is scheduled.

You receive the structured intake record first. If your firm enables recordings or transcripts, those can be reviewed according to your retention policy.

Stage 2 — LEVERAGE

Structure Case Value

Most solo and small PI firms don't lose cases. They lose value inside the case. LEVERAGE is the case-structure layer between intake and settlement. It helps attorneys calculate damages, track deadline risk, run demand-readiness checks, and organize case facts for attorney review.

What LEVERAGE does

  • Calculates damages from attorney-provided inputs
  • Tracks deadline-risk signals based on configured jurisdiction rules
  • Runs demand-readiness checks before attorney review
  • Organizes structured case facts without generating legal demand text
  • Supports attorney-controlled case preparation

What LEVERAGE is not

LEVERAGE is not case management software. It is not a strategy recommendation engine. It does not tell you what a case is worth. It structures the information you already have — and surfaces what you might be missing.

Stage 3 — SETTLE

Confidence-Labeled Settlement Intelligence

When you are preparing a demand or evaluating a settlement offer, SETTLE provides structured settlement ranges with confidence labels — so you know how much weight to give each data point.

How SETTLE works

  • Generates settlement ranges with confidence labels (High / Medium / Low)
  • Draws on available comparable settlement signals, with data sources and confidence clearly labeled
  • Confidence-based billing: no charge if data is insufficient for a reliable range
  • You confirm before paying for low-confidence reports

What SETTLE is not

SETTLE does not guarantee a specific settlement outcome. It does not replace your professional judgment. It provides structured, confidence-labeled signals to support your decision — before you make it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Benjamin an AI chatbot?

No. Benjamin is not a free-form chatbot. It uses deterministic intake controls and pre-approved question paths configured for the firm. It can capture open-ended caller responses where approved, but it does not provide legal advice or make legal judgments.

Does Benjamin record calls?

Recording and transcript generation are optional. If the law firm enables them, audio and transcripts are retained according to the firm's configured retention policy. If disabled, the system relies on structured intake facts, scores, and risk flags as the core business record.

How does lead priority work?

Every intake record remains visible inside the portal. Benjamin may flag A+ opportunities based on firm-approved criteria, but the firm reviews and decides which opportunities to pursue. TrueVow INTAKE does not use public per-lead unlock fees.

How long does setup take?

After approval and plan selection, setup timing depends on routing, calendar access, intake configuration, recording policy, and firm readiness. We limit onboarding to maintain setup quality. You will configure your intake path, connect your calendar, and complete a test call before going live.

Do I have to use all three stages?

No. INTAKE works as a standalone product. LEVERAGE can be used standalone or at the ecosystem price with active INTAKE. SETTLE Per Case is available to approved firms, while SETTLE Pro requires active INTAKE or LEVERAGE. Many firms start with INTAKE and add stages as their pipeline matures.

What practice areas does Benjamin support?

Benjamin currently supports Personal Injury intake with practice-specific qualification scripts. Additional practice areas are being developed. Check with us during the application process for the latest availability.

What is the ROI?

ROI depends on your call volume, consultation conversion rate, and average case fee. We provide a directional ROI calculator that lets you estimate opportunity captured based on your own numbers. This is a directional estimate only — not a promise, prediction, or guarantee.

How is TrueVow different from broader legal AI platforms?

Broader legal AI platforms may support drafting, discovery, medical summaries, demand letters, or full case lifecycle workflows. TrueVow is intake-first and built for solo and small PI firms.
