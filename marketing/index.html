The Villain Is the After-the-Ring Gap

The After-the-Ring Gap is the space between a caller reaching out and the firm having a structured intake record ready for attorney review. The ad worked. The referral worked. The search result worked. Then the caller reached the firm — and the process depended on voicemail, callbacks, scattered notes, or a generic message-taker.

The caller hears a generic voicemail. The message is delayed, shallow, or never reviewed with enough detail. They may keep searching for help while incident timing, practice-area fit, and consultation booking remain unclear. That is the cost of the After-the-Ring Gap: the firm paid for the call, but may not have a structured intake record ready for attorney review.

What the gap looks like

  • A caller leaves a voicemail after hours
  • A receptionist takes a shallow message
  • Incident timing is not captured clearly
  • Practice-area fit is unclear
  • The consultation is not booked while intent is high

Common Questions

What is TrueVow?

TrueVow is an intake architecture partner for solo and small personal injury firms. TrueVow helps firms map how intake works today, design a controlled routed-call process, implement Benjamin as the voice layer, and give attorneys structured intake records for review. TrueVow is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice.

What does Benjamin do?

Benjamin is the voice layer that answers routed calls when enabled, follows selected intake templates, screens intake fit, creates structured intake records, flags priority opportunities, and books consultations based on configured rules. Benjamin does not provide legal advice, create an attorney-client relationship, run conflicts checks, or decide whether to accept a case.

Is Benjamin an answering service?

No. Answering services usually take messages and forward calls. Benjamin is a controlled intake layer for routed PI calls. It follows selected intake templates, screens intake fit, creates structured intake records, flags priority opportunities, and books consultations based on onboarding settings and configured rules.

Is this a chatbot?

No. Benjamin is not designed to be a free-form legal-advice chatbot. It uses controlled intake logic, selected templates, configured routing rules, and attorney review workflows for live-call intake decisions.

What does it cost?

INTAKE plans start at $499/month. Pricing scales by routed call volume, workflow needs, reporting, and support. There are no public per-lead unlock fees, and all intake records remain visible inside the portal. Full pricing is available on the Pricing page.

Do I pay for every lead?

No. TrueVow INTAKE does not use public per-lead unlock fees. Every plan keeps intake records visible inside the portal. Benjamin may flag priority opportunities based on configured criteria, but the firm reviews and decides what to pursue.

Is Spanish included?

Yes. English and Spanish intake are both included at no extra cost. Callers are served in whichever language they prefer.

Does Benjamin create a case?

No. Benjamin creates structured intake records for attorney review. The firm decides whether an intake opportunity becomes a case, whether conflicts checks are cleared, and whether representation will be offered.

Can I use SETTLE without INTAKE?

SETTLE access may be available to approved firms for settlement-range context with confidence labels. SETTLE does not predict, recommend, or guarantee outcomes. Availability, eligibility, and pricing are confirmed after approval.

Is TrueVow just software?

No. TrueVow combines software, intake mapping, onboarding configuration, routed-call setup, and ongoing support. Benjamin is the voice layer, but the broader product is a controlled intake process for the firm.

Do we need to change our whole firm to use TrueVow?

No. TrueVow starts with the calls and workflows your firm already has: phone routing, calendar booking, intake questions, alerts, and attorney review. No full case-management migration is required to begin.

Does TrueVow customize intake for each firm?

TrueVow uses selected intake templates and onboarding settings. Standard configuration is included during onboarding. Custom workflows, unusual routing, or complex integrations may require separate scoping, validation, and pricing.
