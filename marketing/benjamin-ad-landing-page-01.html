What Benjamin Does on a Routed Call

Benjamin is not a generic receptionist and not a free-form legal chatbot. It is controlled intake automation built around your firm’s approved path.

Step 1

Answers routed calls when enabled

Calls route based on your configured schedule, routing rules, and intake setup.

Step 2

Qualifies the matter

Benjamin collects structured facts such as incident type, timing, injury signals, treatment status, location, and urgency.

Step 3

Grades the lead

Qualified leads are organized into grades so the firm can identify which opportunities deserve immediate attention. Lead grades are based on configured intake criteria, not legal advice or a case-value prediction.

Step 4

Books or escalates

Benjamin can help route the next step: consultation booking, priority alert, follow-up queue, or structured review.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Benjamin a chatbot?

No. Benjamin is not a free-form chatbot. Benjamin follows selected intake templates and onboarding settings, routing rules, escalation logic, and recording settings. It can support yes/no questions, structured responses, and approved open-ended prompts, but it is not designed to provide legal advice or make attorney judgment calls.

Does Benjamin answer every call?

Benjamin answers routed calls when enabled based on your firm’s configured schedule, routing rules, and intake setup. It should not be described as guaranteed universal call capture.

Can Benjamin handle Spanish callers?

Yes. Spanish intake support is included with INTAKE plans and is not sold as a separate add-on.

Do you record calls or generate transcripts?

Only when enabled by the firm. Recording and transcript generation are optional, controlled by firm policy, and configured during onboarding.

Can I buy directly from this page?

No. TrueVow uses an approval-first process. Apply first, get reviewed, then activate and pay inside the customer portal if approved.

Is this only for personal injury firms?

This page is focused on solo and small plaintiff-side PI firms. Additional practice areas may be reviewed during onboarding, but the public INTAKE workflow is optimized around PI intake and qualification.

What does lead grading mean?

Lead grades are based on configured intake criteria such as incident type, injury signals, treatment status, timing, location, and firm rules. They are not legal advice, case-value predictions, or guarantees of case quality.
