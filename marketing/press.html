PRESS RELEASE

Intakely.ai Discontinues AI-Powered Intake Service Following Attorney Feedback on Bar Compliance Concerns

Intakely Technologies announced today the immediate discontinuation of its AI-powered legal intake service, Intakely.ai, following extensive beta testing feedback from solo and small law firm attorneys.

During a 6-month beta period (August 2024 - January 2025), attorneys raised critical concerns about AI compliance risks, data privacy, and the inability to audit or defend AI decision-making to state Bar associations.

"Our beta testers told us: 'I can't risk recording client calls,' 'AI hallucinated and gave wrong statute deadlines,' and 'I can't defend a black box to the Bar.' They were right. We're shutting down Intakely.ai effective immediately and going back to the drawing board."
— Afghan S. Yasha Ullah Khan, Founder & CEO, Intakely Technologies

Key concerns raised by attorneys during beta:

  • AI hallucinations giving incorrect legal information to callers
  • Inability to prove what AI stored or accessed during calls
  • Call recordings creating malpractice liability and Bar audit risks
  • AI started giving fee estimates and legal advice despite guardrails (unauthorized practice of law)
  • No audit trail defensible to state Bar associations

Rather than force attorneys to adopt a system they didn't trust, the company chose to discontinue the service entirely and explore alternative approaches that prioritize attorney safety and Bar compliance over speed-to-market.

All beta testing was provided at no cost. The company plans to spend Q1-Q2 2025 researching deterministic, non-AI alternatives for legal intake automation.
