Frequently Asked Questions
Is LEVERAGE case management software?

No. Case management software organizes files, tracks deadlines, and stores documents. LEVERAGE is the case value discipline layer — it identifies missing damages, structures your position, and gives you negotiation playbooks. They solve fundamentally different problems.

Case management tells you where a file is. LEVERAGE helps surface what may be missing before value is negotiated.
How does LEVERAGE work with INTAKE?

If you use INTAKE, case details auto-populate into LEVERAGE — no manual re-entry. You also get the bundled ecosystem price ($79/mo Solo, $279/mo Growth, $699/mo Team instead of standalone pricing).

LEVERAGE also works as a standalone product. You can enter case details manually if you don't use INTAKE.

How does LEVERAGE connect to SETTLE?

LEVERAGE structures your case for a clearer position. SETTLE provides settlement range context before you accept an offer. After you use LEVERAGE to build your position, SETTLE checks the settlement range against comparable data.

Together, they help close the gap between what you ask for and what the available range context may support.

What does "damage identification" mean?

Many demand letters can under-document recoverable damages — future medical costs, loss of earning capacity, cognitive rehabilitation, pain and suffering multipliers. LEVERAGE scans your case for damages you may have overlooked, based on comparable case patterns and injury type.

Is LEVERAGE legal advice?

No. LEVERAGE outputs are decision-support tools, not legal advice, settlement guarantees, or outcome predictions. You stay in control of every case decision. LEVERAGE gives you structure. You make the call.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. No long-term contracts. All LEVERAGE tiers are month-to-month. Cancel anytime through the customer portal.

What does "public-pattern insights" include?

Available on the Growth and Team tiers. LEVERAGE may surface jurisdictional or party-pattern signals from public records where available — negotiation patterns, typical offer ranges, and observed settlement behavior. It does not rely on confidential information and does not guarantee behavior in any case.

Is LEVERAGE independent of insurers?

LEVERAGE is designed as attorney-controlled decision support. It is not an insurer-controlled product. It may surface jurisdictional or party-pattern signals from public records where available, but attorneys remain responsible for case strategy, settlement judgment, and client advice.
