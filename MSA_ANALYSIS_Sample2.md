### **#11 - TrueVow CONNECT (Referral Network)**

**What it is:**  
Comprehensive definition of referral network service with detailed legal disclaimers.

**Key provisions:**

**Definition (Section 1):**
> "TrueVow CONNECT means the service that facilitates the referral of a Customer's leads or clients to other practicing law firms or medical providers within the TrueVow network. Participation may allow the referring Customer to earn a referral fee, subject to specific terms, conditions, and eligibility requirements."

**Functional Section (Section 3.9):**
- **3.9.1 Referral Service** - Platform facilitates connections between Referrer and Referee
- **3.9.2 Referrer's Responsibilities:**
  - Ethical compliance (fee splitting, conflicts, client consent, UPL)
  - Client consent (written where required)
  - No guarantee of acceptance or payment
- **3.9.3 Referee's Obligations** - Conflicts checks, case merit, direct agreement with Referrer
- **3.9.4 Role of TrueVow** - "Acts solely as an introductory platform," not a party to referral agreements
- **3.9.5 Fee Disputes** - Strictly between Referrer and Referee

**Indemnification Enhancement (Section 10.2):**
> "Any referral made through TrueVow CONNECT, including any violation of bar rules regarding fee splitting, client consent, or unauthorized practice of law"

**Customer Responsibilities (Section 8.2):**
> "Customer is solely liable for all communications, content, and data management within TrueVow CONNECT. Customer must obtain all necessary consents for communications and ensure compliance with telemarketing, spam, and data protection laws (e.g., TCPA, CAN-SPAM, GDPR)."

**Where to add:**  
- Definition in Section 1
- New Section 3.9 (TrueVow CONNECT)
- Update Section 8 (Customer Responsibilities)
- Update Section 10.2 (Customer Indemnification)

**Why valuable:**  
- Opens new revenue stream (referral network)
- Shields Provider from ethical violations by attorneys
- Clear allocation of compliance burden to Customer
- "Mere conduit" defense for communications

### **#12 - TrueVow SETTLE (Data Contribution Model)**

**What it is:**  
Crowd-sourced data platform allowing Customers to contribute anonymized settlement data.

**Key provisions:**

**Enhanced Definition (Section 1.18):**
> "TrueVow SETTLE means the service for visualising historical settlement benchmarks, generating settlement reports, and contributing to an anonymized, aggregated database of case outcomes."

**New Section 3.8 (Anonymous Database & Data Contribution):**

**3.8.1 Functionality:**
> "TrueVow SETTLE displays historical settlement ranges derived from public dockets and, separately, from anonymized data voluntarily contributed by Customers. It does not predict future outcomes and is designed to level the playing field against proprietary insurer databases."

**3.8.2 No PHI:**
> "The service is architected to store no Protected Health Information (PHI) or client identifiers."

**3.8.3 Data Contribution:**
- **(a) Voluntary Contribution** - Customers may contribute anonymized case outcome data
- **(b) Anonymization** - Customer must strip all PII, PHI, client names, law firm names before contribution
- **(c) License Grant** - "By contributing anonymized data, Customer grants TrueVow a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide license to use, reproduce, modify, and create derivative works from the anonymized data for the purpose of operating, improving, and providing the TrueVow SETTLE service."
- **(d) No Duty to Contribute** - Access not contingent on data contribution

**Indemnification Enhancement (Section 10.2(v)):**
> "The contribution of non-anonymized data to TrueVow SETTLE"

**Where to add:**  
- Update Definition 1.18
- New Section 3.8 (TrueVow SETTLE)
- Update Section 4.1 (Provider IP - add "Aggregated Statistics")
- Update Section 10.2 (Customer Indemnification)

**Why valuable:**  
- Network effects (more data = more value)
- Competitive moat against insurance companies
- Perpetual license to contributed data protects platform
- Indemnification for non-anonymized data shields Provider
