## CRITICAL FINDINGS

### **1. Three-Document Framework Required**
The samples reveal that bar compliance requires **three distinct documents**:
1. **Attorney-Only Compliance Guide** (detailed, checklist-based)
2. **Bar Submission Memorandum** (formal, persuasive)
3. **Public-Facing Ethics Statement** (website content)

The baseline lacks this structure.

### **2. Module-Specific Compliance is Non-Negotiable**
INTAKE, DRAFT, SETTLE, CONNECT, and VERIFY each trigger **different ethical rules**. A generic compliance statement is insufficient and legally vulnerable.

### **3. State-Specific Addenda are Essential**
At minimum, need dedicated sections for: CA, NY, FL, TX, IL (representing 60%+ of U.S. legal market).

### **4. Client Disclosure is a Separate Deliverable**
The mandatory client disclosure section should be:
- Extractable as a standalone document
- Ready to insert into engagement letters
- Written in plain English (not legalese)

### **5. Operational Checklists Drive Compliance**
The checkbox format ("☐ Do this") is **far more valuable** than narrative explanations. Attorneys want actionable to-dos.
