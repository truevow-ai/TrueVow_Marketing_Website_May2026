| Small firm (5–50 active cases) | **Top-3 requirement** | Manual data entry becomes bottleneck |

### Case Management Adoption

- **65–75%** of PI firms use case management software
- Leading PI platforms: Clio Manage, Filevine, CASEpeer, SmartAdvocate, Needles, Litify, Smokeball
- PI adoption is higher than general practice because PI is document-intensive and deadline-driven

### ROI / Reporting Awareness

- **Only 20–30%** of solo PI firms actively track cost-per-signed-case or channel-level ROI with rigor
- The majority evaluate performance by "are we busy enough?" — a gut-feel metric
- Most firms track only 1–2 lagging indicators (signed cases, cost per new case) and zero leading indicators (speed to contact, contact rate, qualification rate)

**Implication:** TrueVow's reporting should be dead simple: "Here's how many calls came in. Here's how many became consults. Here's your cost per consult." Don't over-engineer — these buyers don't use complex analytics.

### Typical PI Consultation Duration

- Initial phone qualification: 5–15 min (done by intake staff or attorney)
- Substantive consultation: **30–60 min** (standard). Free. 60 min for in-person; 30 min for phone.

### Consults Per Month (Solo PI)

- Typical solo: **5–15 new consults/week** (20–60/month)
- At $5,000/mo ad spend: **12–20 qualified consults/month** (from 30–50 calls)

### TrueVow Impact: Additional Attorney Hours

If TrueVow books 6–10 *additional* consults/month:
- Additional consult time: 6–10 × 30–60 min = **3–10 hours/month**
- Plus prep/follow-up: ~30 min per consult = **3–5 hours/month**
- **Total: ~6–15 additional attorney hours/month**

At $250–$350/hr opportunity cost = $1,500–$5,250 in attorney time. Offset by value of new signed cases (~$10,230 in fees per case at avg $31K settlement × 33%).

## SECTION J: THE PSYCHOLOGICAL SELF-SELL FORMULA

### The Problem with "Value Differentiation"

The 40–55% of solos who self-answer are **not comparing TrueVow to Smith.ai.** They are comparing TrueVow to "just keep answering the phone myself" — which is free. No amount of "deterministic intake logic" or "A+ qualification algorithms" converts this cohort. They don't speak that language. 59% don't budget for technology at all.

### What Actually Makes a Self-Sell

| Trigger | Why It Works | ICP Data Point |
|---|---|---|
| **"You're losing $1,900/mo in ad spend to voicemail"** | Concrete, calculable, immediately verifiable. Their own number, not ours. | 38% voicemail abandonment × $5,000/mo ad spend = $1,900/mo wasted. |
| **"$499/mo vs. $4,000/mo for a receptionist"** | They know what a receptionist costs. The anchor is real in their financial world. | Receptionist TCO: $48,000/yr ($4,000/mo). Documented. |
| **"No per-lead billing. No per-minute billing. One price."** | Directly addresses fear #2 (unpredictable billing). Anti-Smith.ai positioning: Smith.ai charges $11.50/call over 30. TrueVow: one flat price. | Smith.ai per-call pricing publicly visible. TrueVow flat = differentiation. |
| **"Try it. Hear Benjamin answer a live PI call. 8 minutes."** | They don't need to understand AI. They need to hear it work. Demo > explain. | Behavioral: "show, don't tell" converts higher than feature lists. |
| **"You take a weekend. Benjamin answers."** | Not a feature — an emotional outcome. Addresses burnout directly. | 28% depression, 19% anxiety in legal profession. 50–60+ hour weeks. Always on call. |

### The Self-Sell Page Flow

1. **Pain:** "38% of your ad spend goes to voicemail. That's $1,900/mo." (Make them feel the leak.)
2. **Demo:** "Hear Benjamin answer a PI call — 8 minutes." (Show, don't tell.)
3. **Price:** "One price. All records visible. No per-lead billing. $499/mo." (No jargon.)
4. **Outcome:** "You take a weekend. Benjamin answers." (Emotional close.)

**Nowhere on the page:** "deterministic logic," "AI-powered," "machine learning," "A+ scoring engine," "unlock methodology."
