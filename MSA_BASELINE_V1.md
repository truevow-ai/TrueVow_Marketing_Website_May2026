## **2. SCOPE OF SERVICES**

### **2.1 Services Provided**

Provider shall deliver the following services to Customer:

**(a) TrueVow Intake™**
- 24/7/365 live phone answering by trained intake specialists
- Deterministic FSM-based qualification (no AI/ML in decision logic)
- Calendar integration and consultation booking
- Bilingual support (English/Spanish)
- SMS/email confirmation to qualified leads
- Real-time dashboard notifications

**(b) TrueVow Draft™**
- Template-based document assembly
- State-specific form libraries (50-state coverage)
- Demand letter generation
- Retainer agreement customization
- E-signature integration

**(c) TrueVow Settle™**
- Historical settlement data visualization
- Jurisdiction-specific insights
- Injury type and case value filters
- Data derived exclusively from public court records
- No predictive modeling or case valuation

**(d) TrueVow Verify™**
- Blockchain-anchored compliance certificates
- OpenTimestamps proof of zero-knowledge architecture
- Immutable audit trail for regulatory compliance
- Shareable verification URLs

### **2.2 Service Delivery Standards**

Provider shall:
- Maintain 99.5% uptime (excluding scheduled maintenance)
- Answer inbound calls within 30 seconds (90th percentile)
- Complete intake qualification within 10 minutes average
- Deliver Qualified Bookings to Customer dashboard within 60 seconds
- Provide 24/7 technical support via email and chat
- Conduct scheduled maintenance during non-peak hours (2-6 AM ET) with 48-hour notice

### **2.3 Service Limitations**

Provider does NOT:
- Provide legal advice or practice law
- Evaluate case merit or legal viability
- Make binding representations to callers
- Guarantee lead conversion or client retention
- File court documents or represent clients
- Store PHI, SSNs, credit card data, or medical records (except as opted-in per Section 6.2)

### **3.3 Qualified Booking Criteria**

A Booking is "Qualified" only if ALL of the following are met:
1. Caller confirms injury occurred within statute of limitations
2. Caller confirms incident occurred within Customer's licensed jurisdiction(s)
3. Caller confirms practice area matches Customer's intake criteria
4. Caller provides valid contact information (name, phone, email)
5. Caller consents to contact and consultation
6. Caller selects and confirms a consultation appointment time
7. Caller is not a duplicate of an existing lead within 90 days

### **3.4 Invoicing and Payment**

### **3.5 Disputed Charges**

## **SCHEDULE A: SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)**

### **A.1 Uptime Commitment**

**99.5% monthly uptime** (excluding scheduled maintenance)

**Calculation:**  
Uptime % = (Total Minutes in Month - Downtime Minutes) / Total Minutes in Month × 100

**Scheduled Maintenance:** Up to 4 hours per month, conducted 2-6 AM ET with 48-hour notice, excluded from calculation.

### **A.2 Performance Metrics**

| **Metric** | **Target** | **Measurement** |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Call Answer Time | 30 seconds | 90th percentile |
| Intake Duration | 10 minutes | Average |
| Booking Delivery | 60 seconds | 95th percentile |
| Support Response Time | 4 hours | Business hours |
| Dashboard Load Time | 2 seconds | 95th percentile |

### **A.3 Service Credits**

If Provider fails to meet the 99.5% uptime commitment:

| **Monthly Uptime %** | **Service Credit** |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| 99.0% - 99.49% | 10% of monthly fees |
| 98.0% - 98.99% | 25% of monthly fees |
| < 98.0% | 50% of monthly fees |

**How to Claim:**  
Customer must submit a written claim within **15 days** of the end of the month. Credits applied to next invoice. **Service credits are Customer's sole remedy for SLA breaches.**

### **A.4 Exclusions from SLA**

The following do NOT count as downtime:
- Scheduled maintenance (with 48-hour notice)
- Customer's internet, hardware, or software failures
- Force majeure events
- Third-party API outages beyond Provider's control
- Customer's breach of Acceptable Use Policy
