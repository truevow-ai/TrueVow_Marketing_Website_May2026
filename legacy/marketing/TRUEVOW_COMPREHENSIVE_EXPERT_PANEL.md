## 🧠 PSYCHOLOGY & DECISION-MAKING EXPERTS

### Behavioral Economics & Decision Science

1. **Daniel Kahneman** (Nobel Prize Winner, "Thinking, Fast and Slow")
   - **Focus:** System 1 vs System 2 thinking, cognitive biases, loss aversion
   - **Key Principle:** People make decisions emotionally (System 1), then justify rationally (System 2)

2. **Richard Thaler** (Nobel Prize Winner, "Nudge")
   - **Focus:** Behavioral economics, choice architecture, defaults
   - **Key Principle:** Small changes in how choices are presented dramatically affect decisions

3. **Robert Cialdini** ("Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion")
   - **Focus:** 6 principles of influence (reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity)
   - **Key Principle:** Scarcity creates urgency ("Limited to 2,100 members")

4. **Dan Ariely** ("Predictably Irrational")
   - **Focus:** Behavioral economics, irrational decision-making patterns
   - **Key Principle:** People are predictably irrational - use anchoring, decoy effect, etc.

5. **Amos Tversky** (Co-founder of Behavioral Economics)
   - **Focus:** Prospect theory, loss aversion, framing effects
   - **Key Principle:** Losses loom larger than gains (loss framing more powerful)

6. **Barry Schwartz** ("The Paradox of Choice")
   - **Focus:** Too many choices = decision paralysis
   - **Key Principle:** 2-3 options optimal, more creates paralysis

7. **Sheena Iyengar** ("The Art of Choosing")
   - **Focus:** Choice architecture, cultural differences in decision-making
   - **Key Principle:** How choices are presented matters more than what choices exist

8. **Dan Gilbert** ("Stumbling on Happiness")
   - **Focus:** Affective forecasting, decision-making errors
   - **Key Principle:** People are bad at predicting what will make them happy

9. **Gerd Gigerenzer** ("Risk Savvy")
   - **Focus:** Heuristics, fast and frugal decision-making
   - **Key Principle:** Simple rules often beat complex analysis

10. **Jonathan Haidt** ("The Righteous Mind")
    - **Focus:** Moral psychology, emotional vs rational decision-making
    - **Key Principle:** Intuition comes first, strategic reasoning second

11. **Malcolm Gladwell** ("Blink", "The Tipping Point")
    - **Focus:** Rapid cognition, thin-slicing, social epidemics
    - **Key Principle:** First impressions matter, small changes create big effects
