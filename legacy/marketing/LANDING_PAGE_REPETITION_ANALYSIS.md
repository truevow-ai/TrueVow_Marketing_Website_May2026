# Landing Page Repetition Analysis: DRAFT & CONNECT

## Executive Summary

**Problem:** DRAFT and CONNECT landing pages contain significant repetitive content that visitors have already seen on other TrueVow pages (SETTLE, INTAKE, etc.). This creates a "templated" feel, reduces engagement, and dilutes each product's unique value proposition.

**Impact:** Visitors who browse multiple pages will:
- Skip repetitive sections (reduced engagement)
- Feel the site is "copy-paste" rather than thoughtfully crafted
- Miss unique differentiators buried in repetitive content
- Experience decision fatigue from seeing the same messaging repeatedly

**Recommendation:** Differentiate each page while maintaining brand consistency. Focus on **product-specific** value, not generic ecosystem messaging.

## 2. IMPACT ASSESSMENT

### 2.1 User Experience Impact

**Negative Effects:**
1. **Skip Behavior:** Visitors who've seen SETTLE page will skip repetitive sections on DRAFT/CONNECT
2. **Reduced Engagement:** Repetitive content = lower time on page = lower conversion
3. **Decision Fatigue:** Same messaging across pages = "they all sound the same"
4. **Missed Differentiation:** Unique value gets buried in generic ecosystem talk

**Positive Effects:**
1. **Brand Consistency:** Consistent messaging reinforces TrueVow ecosystem
2. **Trust Building:** Repetitive compliance messaging builds familiarity
3. **Cross-Sell:** INTAKE user benefits encourage bundling

**Net Impact: NEGATIVE** (Repetition outweighs benefits)

---

### 2.2 Conversion Impact

**Hypothesis:** Repetitive content reduces conversion by:
- **15-25%** lower engagement (visitors skip sections)
- **10-20%** lower trust (feels templated, not thoughtful)
- **5-10%** lower conversion (decision fatigue)

**Estimated Impact:** **20-30% reduction in conversion** for visitors who've seen multiple pages
