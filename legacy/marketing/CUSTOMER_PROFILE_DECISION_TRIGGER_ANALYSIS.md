# Customer Profile & Decision-Making Trigger Analysis
## DRAFT & CONNECT Landing Pages

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Current State:** Pages address general "attorneys" but don't explicitly segment by customer profile or decision-making style.

**Gap:** Missing persona-specific messaging and decision triggers for different customer types.

**Recommendation:** Add persona-specific sections and decision triggers to increase conversion across all customer profiles.

---

## 1. CUSTOMER PROFILES (PERSONAS)

### 1.1 Primary Personas

#### Persona A: "The Risk-Averse Solo" (DRAFT Primary)
**Profile:**
- Solo PI attorney (1 attorney)
- 5-15 years experience
- Has experienced a compliance error or knows someone who has
- **Decision Style:** Risk-averse, fear-driven, compliance-focused
- **Pain Points:** Malpractice anxiety, license protection, case dismissal fear
- **Triggers:** Loss aversion (Kahneman), Authority (Cialdini), Risk reversal
- **Current Coverage:** ✅ Good (loss framing, compliance focus)
- **Gap:** Could be more explicit about "license protection" and "malpractice prevention"

#### Persona B: "The Efficiency-Seeker" (DRAFT Secondary)
**Profile:**
- Solo or small firm (2-5 attorneys)
- 3-10 years experience
- Tech-savvy, efficiency-focused
- **Decision Style:** ROI-focused, time-conscious, efficiency-driven
- **Pain Points:** Time waste, manual processes, inefficiency
- **Triggers:** Value stacking (Hormozi), Time savings, ROI calculations
- **Current Coverage:** ⚠️ Moderate (ROI calculator exists but could be more prominent)
- **Gap:** Time savings not emphasized enough in hero section

#### Persona C: "The Revenue Maximizer" (CONNECT Primary)
**Profile:**
- Solo or small firm (1-10 attorneys)
- 5-20 years experience
- Growth-oriented, revenue-focused
- **Decision Style:** Opportunity-driven, revenue-focused, network-building
- **Pain Points:** Lost referral fees, turning away cases, limited network
- **Triggers:** Loss aversion (lost revenue), Opportunity framing, Social proof (network size)
- **Current Coverage:** ✅ Good (revenue loss framing)
- **Gap:** Could emphasize "network expansion" and "passive income" more

#### Persona D: "The Capacity Manager" (CONNECT Secondary)
**Profile:**
- Small firm (6-21 attorneys)
- Managing partner or senior attorney
- Capacity-constrained, strategic
- **Decision Style:** Strategic, capacity-focused, delegation-oriented
- **Pain Points:** At capacity, can't take more cases, need to refer overflow
- **Triggers:** Capacity relief, Strategic advantage, Delegation benefits
- **Current Coverage:** ⚠️ Weak (not explicitly addressed)
- **Gap:** Missing "capacity management" messaging

#### Persona E: "The Multi-Practice Attorney" (Both Products)
**Profile:**
- Solo or small firm
- Handles multiple practice areas (PI + Family Law, etc.)
- **Decision Style:** Versatility-focused, cross-practice efficiency
- **Pain Points:** Different compliance rules, different referral needs
- **Triggers:** Versatility, Cross-practice efficiency, One-tool solution
- **Current Coverage:** ⚠️ Moderate (practice areas mentioned but not as primary persona)
- **Gap:** Not positioned as primary benefit for multi-practice attorneys

#### Persona F: "The INTAKE User" (Both Products - Existing Customer)
**Profile:**
- Already using TrueVow INTAKE
- Trusts TrueVow, values integration
- **Decision Style:** Trust-based, integration-focused, convenience-driven
- **Pain Points:** Need seamless integration, want to complete workflow
- **Triggers:** Trust (existing relationship), Convenience, Value stacking
- **Current Coverage:** ✅ Good (INTAKE user sections exist)
- **Gap:** Could emphasize "seamless integration" and "zero setup" more

#### Persona G: "The Founding Member" (Both Products - Early Adopter)
**Profile:**
- Early adopter, co-builder mindset
- Values exclusivity, wants to shape product
- **Decision Style:** Exclusivity-driven, co-creation focused, community-oriented
- **Pain Points:** Want to be part of something, want exclusive benefits
- **Triggers:** Scarcity (Cialdini), Exclusivity, Co-creation opportunity
- **Current Coverage:** ⚠️ Moderate (mentioned but not emphasized)
- **Gap:** Could emphasize "co-builder" and "exclusive access" more

## 7. KEY INSIGHTS

1. **Different personas need different triggers:** Risk-averse attorneys respond to loss framing, efficiency-seekers respond to ROI, revenue maximizers respond to opportunity framing.

2. **Decision stage matters:** Awareness stage needs problem framing, consideration stage needs comparison, decision stage needs objection handling.

3. **Missing urgency and scarcity:** Both pages could benefit from stronger urgency and scarcity triggers.

4. **Persona-specific sections:** Adding explicit persona sections will help visitors self-identify and see themselves in the solution.

5. **Objection handling:** Missing explicit objection handling reduces conversion at decision stage.
