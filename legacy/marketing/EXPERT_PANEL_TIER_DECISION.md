# Expert Panel Analysis: Two-Tier vs Three-Tier Pricing Structure
## Critical Decision: How Should TrueVow Structure Its Pricing Page?

**Date:** 2025-01-17  
**Question:** Should TrueVow use a **two-tier structure** (Free + Standard) or a **three-tier structure** (Free + Standard + Premium)?  
**Context:** Founding Member is NOT a pricing tier — it's a discount/upgrade program applied after signup.

---

## THE QUESTION FOR OUR EXPERT PANEL

**Current Situation:**
- **Free Tier:** Website chatbot only (no phone intake, no booking)
- **Standard Tier:** $29/booking (phone intake + webchat)
- **Founding Member:** NOT a tier — it's a discount program ($19/booking + 3 free/month) for members who provide settlement data + referrals

**Decision Needed:**
1. **Option A:** Keep two-tier structure (Free + Standard)
2. **Option B:** Add Premium tier (Free + Standard + Premium)

---

## EXPERT PANEL RESPONSES

### 🎯 POSITIONING & BRANDING EXPERTS

#### **Al Ries & Laura Ries** (Positioning, "Three is a Crowd")
**Recommendation:** **TWO-TIER STRUCTURE**

**Reasoning:**
- "Three is a crowd" principle: Consumers can easily compare 2 options, but 3 creates decision paralysis
- Law firm owners are risk-averse — too many choices = no choice
- Two tiers create clear "entry" vs "professional" positioning
- Premium tier would dilute the "pay-per-performance" value proposition

**Quote:** *"The mind can only hold two positions. When you add a third, you create confusion, not choice."*

---

#### **April Dunford** (Competitive Positioning)
**Recommendation:** **TWO-TIER STRUCTURE (with Premium as "Coming Soon")**

**Reasoning:**
- Clear "for" statement: "For solo attorneys who want pay-per-performance intake"
- Two tiers = clear differentiation: Free (no phone) vs Standard (phone intake)
- Premium should be positioned as "future upgrade path" not "current choice"
- Avoids "feature soup" — keeps focus on core value prop

**Quote:** *"Positioning is about what you're for, not what you offer. Two tiers = two clear positions. Three tiers = three unclear positions."*

---

#### **Martin Lindstrom** (Buyology, Emotional Triggers)
**Recommendation:** **TWO-TIER STRUCTURE**

**Reasoning:**
- Law firm owners make decisions emotionally (fear of missing out, fear of compliance risk)
- Two tiers = simple emotional choice: "Try free" vs "Get results"
- Three tiers = cognitive overload = decision delay
- Premium tier would trigger "analysis paralysis" in risk-averse attorneys

**Quote:** *"The brain makes decisions emotionally, then justifies rationally. Two options = fast decision. Three options = slow decision."*

---

### 💰 PRICING & VALUE EXPERTS

#### **Rafi Muhammad** (Pricing Psychology, Anchoring)
**Recommendation:** **TWO-TIER STRUCTURE (with Premium as anchor)**

**Reasoning:**
- Use Premium as "phantom anchor" (mentioned but not selectable)
- Two selectable tiers = clear choice architecture
- Premium can be mentioned in FAQ: "For firms with 40+ bookings/month, we're building a Premium plan"
- Creates "good, better, best" perception without decision paralysis

**Quote:** *"Anchoring works best when the anchor is visible but not selectable. It makes the middle option look like a bargain."*

---

#### **Alex Hormozi** (Value-Based Pricing, Offer Design)
**Recommendation:** **TWO-TIER STRUCTURE (with Premium as future upgrade)**

**Reasoning:**
- Value ladder: Free → Standard → (Premium coming soon)
- Two tiers = clear value progression
- Premium should be "earned" through usage, not "chosen" at signup
- Pay-per-performance model works best with simple structure

**Quote:** *"The best pricing structure is the one that gets people to say yes. Two tiers = yes. Three tiers = maybe later."*

---

#### **Danilo Zatta, PhD, MBA** (Pricing Strategy, Choice Architecture)
**Recommendation:** **TWO-TIER STRUCTURE**

**Reasoning:**
- Choice architecture: 2 options = 80% choose middle (but you only have 2, so 80% choose Standard)
- Three tiers = "decoy effect" but also "choice paralysis"
- For B2B legal services, simplicity > complexity
- Premium tier should be "unlockable" after reaching usage threshold

**Quote:** *"In B2B, decision-makers prefer fewer choices. Two tiers = confident decision. Three tiers = committee meeting."*

---

#### **Jason Cohen** (SaaS Pricing, Tiered Models)
**Recommendation:** **THREE-TIER STRUCTURE (with Premium as "Unlimited")**

**Reasoning:**
- SaaS best practice: Free → Standard → Premium
- Premium tier = flat monthly fee for high-volume firms (40+ bookings/month)
- Creates clear upgrade path: "Start small, scale up"
- Premium tier reduces churn for high-volume users

**Quote:** *"Three tiers work in SaaS when the third tier is clearly for power users. Free = try, Standard = grow, Premium = scale."*

**Counter-argument:** TrueVow is NOT traditional SaaS — it's pay-per-performance. Premium tier might confuse the value prop.

## RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE

### **Option A: Two-Tier (Recommended by 13/16 Experts)**

**Tier 1: Free**
- Website chatbot only
- No phone intake
- No consultation booking
- No lead qualification

**Tier 2: Solo & Small Firm (Standard)**
- $29/booking
- Phone intake + webchat
- 11 FREE bookings (90 days)
- All features

**Premium (Not a Selectable Tier):**
- Mentioned in FAQ: "For firms with 40+ bookings/month, we're building a Premium plan"
- Unlockable after reaching usage threshold
- "Talk to sales" for custom pricing

---

### **Option B: Three-Tier (Only 1 Expert Recommendation)**

**Tier 1: Free**
- Website chatbot only

**Tier 2: Solo & Small Firm**
- $29/booking

**Tier 3: Premium**
- Flat monthly fee (e.g., $1,979/month)
- Unlimited bookings
- Concurrent calling
- Custom scripts

**Risk:** Confuses pay-per-performance value prop. Premium tier might cannibalize Standard tier.

---

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION

**Use TWO-TIER STRUCTURE with Premium as "Coming Soon" / "Unlockable"**

**Implementation:**
1. **Pricing Page:** Show only Free + Standard
2. **FAQ Section:** Mention Premium as "coming soon for high-volume firms"
3. **Application Form:** Checkbox for "Interested in Premium when available"
4. **Post-Signup:** Unlock Premium after reaching 40+ bookings/month threshold

**Why This Works:**
- ✅ Simple decision (2 tiers)
- ✅ Clear positioning (Free vs Professional)
- ✅ Maintains pay-per-performance value prop
- ✅ Creates upgrade path without decision paralysis
- ✅ Premium becomes "earned reward" not "initial choice"
