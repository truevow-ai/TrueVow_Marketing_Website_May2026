## 3. ABA MODEL RULE 5.3 COMPLIANCE (NON-LAWYER ASSISTANCE)

### 3.1 Rule 5.3 Overview
ABA Model Rule 5.3 governs a lawyer's responsibilities when utilizing the services of non-lawyers. The rule requires that:
- The lawyer must ensure the non-lawyer's conduct is compatible with the lawyer's professional obligations
- The lawyer retains responsibility for supervising the non-lawyer
- The lawyer is responsible for the non-lawyer's conduct if it would constitute a violation of the Rules of Professional Conduct

### 3.2 TrueVow's Compliance Framework

**a) Supervisory Responsibility:**
- TrueVow provides **tools and services** that the attorney **supervises and controls**
- The attorney configures intake scripts, qualification questions, and case acceptance criteria
- The attorney reviews all output (bookings, documents, reports) before use
- The attorney retains full discretion to accept or reject any lead, document, or recommendation

**b) No Substitution of Professional Judgment:**
- TrueVow **does not evaluate** the legal merits of any claim
- TrueVow **does not advise** callers on whether they have a valid case
- TrueVow **does not make** case acceptance decisions
- TrueVow **does not provide** legal advice to callers or clients

**c) Attorney Control:**
- The attorney configures all intake scripts and qualification logic
- The attorney defines all qualification criteria (e.g., statute of limitations, case types, jurisdictions)
- The attorney can override, modify, or reject any output
- The attorney maintains full control over client communications

**d) Clear Role Boundaries:**
- TrueVow's role is limited to **administrative and technical support**
- All legal determinations remain with the attorney
- All client-facing legal communications are the attorney's responsibility

## 4. ABA MODEL RULE 5.4 COMPLIANCE (FEE SPLITTING & PROFESSIONAL INDEPENDENCE)

### 4.1 Rule 5.4 Overview
ABA Model Rule 5.4 prohibits:
- Sharing legal fees with non-lawyers (with limited exceptions)
- Forming partnerships with non-lawyers if any partnership activity involves the practice of law
- Allowing non-lawyers to direct or control the lawyer's professional judgment

### 4.2 TrueVow's Compliance Framework

**a) No Fee Splitting:**
- TrueVow charges **flat fees for services rendered**, not a percentage of legal fees

- TrueVow **does not** receive any portion of:
  - Attorney contingency fees
  - Client settlements or judgments
  - Retainer fees
  - Hourly billing revenue

**b) No Partnership or Ownership:**
- TrueVow is an **independent technology vendor**
- TrueVow has **no ownership interest** in any law firm
- No law firm has **any ownership interest** in TrueVow
- No profit-sharing or partnership arrangement exists

**c) No Control Over Professional Judgment:**
- TrueVow **does not** direct the attorney on case acceptance decisions
- TrueVow **does not** advise the attorney on legal strategy
- TrueVow **does not** control client communications
- TrueVow **does not** make decisions on behalf of the attorney
- The attorney retains **full and exclusive professional independence**

**d) Service-Based Relationship:**
- TrueVow's relationship with the attorney is **vendor-client**, not partnership
- Fees are for **technology services**, not legal work
- The attorney can terminate the relationship at any time without cause

## 5. ABA MODEL RULE 7.2 COMPLIANCE (ADVERTISING & SOLICITATION)

### 5.1 Rule 7.2 Overview
ABA Model Rule 7.2 governs lawyer advertising and requires that:
- Advertising must not be false or misleading
- Advertising must include the name and contact information of at least one lawyer responsible for its content
- Payments to third parties for client referrals are permitted only under certain conditions

### 5.2 TrueVow's Compliance Framework

**a) Not a Referral Service:**
- TrueVow **does not** solicit clients on behalf of the attorney
- TrueVow **does not** refer clients to the attorney
- TrueVow **does not** advertise legal services to the public on behalf of the attorney

**b) Attorney's Phone Number:**
- All intake calls are to **the attorney's own published phone number**
- Callers are calling **the attorney**, not TrueVow
- TrueVow answers on behalf of the attorney (as an agent) using the attorney's scripts

**c) Attorney Control of Advertising:**
- The attorney retains full control over all advertising content
- The attorney approves all intake scripts and messaging
- The attorney's name and contact information are disclosed to all callers
- All advertising complies with state bar rules (attorney's responsibility)

**d) No Compensation for Referrals:**
- TrueVow is **not paid per client referred**
- TrueVow is paid per **Qualified Booking** (a scheduled consultation), not per client signed
- This is a **service fee**, not a referral fee
- The attorney may choose not to sign a client even after a Qualified Booking (no obligation)

## 7. CONFIDENTIALITY & ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGE

### 7.1 Preservation of Privilege
TrueVow is designed to **preserve**, not waive, attorney-client privilege:
- TrueVow acts as the **attorney's agent** in intake and document assembly
- Information disclosed to TrueVow is disclosed **for the purpose of facilitating legal representation**
- Under the **common-interest doctrine**, such disclosure does not waive privilege
- TrueVow is subject to the same confidentiality obligations as the attorney's employees

### 7.2 Federal Rule of Evidence 502(d) Protection
TrueVow agreements include **Fed. R. Evid. 502(d)** limited waiver provisions:
- Any disclosure of privileged material to TrueVow is **not a general waiver**
- Privilege is preserved for all other purposes
- If privileged material is inadvertently disclosed to a third party, it does not constitute a waiver

### 7.3 Confidentiality Obligations
TrueVow is bound by strict confidentiality obligations:
- All caller data, transcripts, and case details are **Confidential Information**
- TrueVow employees sign confidentiality agreements
- TrueVow sub-processors are bound by Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
- **Default zero-retention policy** (no storage of call audio or transcripts unless attorney opts in)

### 7.4 Subpoena Response
If TrueVow receives a subpoena or legal process demanding disclosure of attorney-client privileged information:
- TrueVow will **promptly notify** the attorney
- TrueVow will **assert all applicable privileges** on the attorney's behalf
- TrueVow will **provide the attorney with a reasonable opportunity** to oppose the disclosure
- TrueVow will not disclose privileged information without a court order or the attorney's consent

### 8.1 Multi-Jurisdictional Practice
TrueVow serves attorneys licensed in all 50 U.S. states. Each state has its own bar rules, which may differ from the ABA Model Rules. **The attorney is solely responsible for ensuring compliance with their state's bar rules.**

TrueVow provides tools to help attorneys comply with state-specific requirements, including:

- Jurisdiction-specific statute of limitations logic
- Customizable qualification criteria
- State-specific document templates

### 8.2 High-Risk Jurisdictions
Some states have stricter rules regarding non-lawyer assistance. Attorneys in these jurisdictions should exercise heightened caution:

**California:**
- Business and Professions Code § 6125 (UPL prohibition)
- Rule 5.3 (supervising non-lawyer assistants)
- TrueVow complies by ensuring all legal determinations remain with the attorney

**New York:**
- 22 NYCRR Part 1200, Rule 5.3
- NY requires attorneys to supervise non-lawyer assistants
- TrueVow complies by providing attorney-configurable tools

**Texas:**
- Texas Disciplinary Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 5.03
- Texas permits non-lawyer assistants under attorney supervision
- TrueVow complies by ensuring attorney control and oversight

**Florida:**
- Florida Bar Rules 4-5.3 and 10-2.1
- Florida has strict UPL rules and referral fee restrictions
- TrueVow complies by charging service fees (not referral fees) and ensuring no legal advice

### 8.3 Attorney's Responsibility
**The attorney must:**
- Review their state's bar rules before using TrueVow
- Configure intake scripts to comply with state advertising rules
- Supervise all TrueVow output and exercise independent professional judgment
- Ensure all client communications comply with state bar rules
- Disclose the use of third-party intake services to clients (if required by state law)

## 11. TECHNOLOGY SAFEGUARDS

### 11.1 Deterministic, Rule-Based Logic (No AI in Legal Functions)
TrueVow's **Core Logic** is **deterministic** and operates via **finite state machines (FSM)**:
- ✅ **No generative AI** or **large language models (LLMs)** in legal functions
- ✅ **No probabilistic inference** or **machine learning** in case qualification
- ✅ **Rule-based only** — the attorney defines the rules, TrueVow executes them

**Ancillary ML Tools** (e.g., speech-to-text, text-to-speech) are used **solely for operational, non-legal purposes**:
- Audio transcription (e.g., Whisper, DeepGram)
- Voice synthesis (e.g., ElevenLabs, Cartesia)
- Voice activity detection (e.g., Silero VAD)

**These tools do not participate in, influence, or affect any legal determination.**

### 11.2 Zero-Knowledge Architecture
By default, TrueVow **does not store**:
- Call audio or transcripts
- Personally identifiable information (PII)
- Protected health information (PHI)
- Social Security Numbers
- Financial account details

**Optional 7-Day Retention:**
- The attorney may opt in to 7-day retention of call recordings and transcripts
- This is **entirely optional** and disabled by default
- **The attorney assumes all risk** for compliance with state recording-consent laws

### 11.3 Data Security & Encryption
- **AES-256-GCM encryption** at rest
- **TLS 1.3 encryption** in transit
- **SHA-256 hashing** for cached data (no plaintext storage)
- **SOC 2 Type II aligned controls** (annual audits)
