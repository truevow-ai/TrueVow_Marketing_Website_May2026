## CRITICAL CLARIFICATION: FOUNDING MEMBER IS NOT A PRICING TIER

**Important:** Founding Member is **NOT** a pricing tier that customers choose on the website.

**What Founding Member Actually Is:**
- A **discount/upgrade program** applied AFTER a customer becomes a paid member
- Requires: (1) Providing anonymized settlement data, (2) Providing referrals (3 attorney referrals/year who convert)
- Only mentioned as a checkbox option in the application form
- **NOT selectable as a pricing tier on the pricing page**

**When reviewing pricing pages:**
- Do NOT treat Founding Member as a pricing tier
- Position it as a loyalty/reward/upgrade program
- Ensure pricing tiers are clear: Free vs Standard (and optionally Premium)
- Founding Member should be in a separate "Earn Discounts" or "Upgrade Program" section

## REVIEW FRAMEWORK

### For Every Landing Page, Evaluate:

#### 1. POSITIONING (Ries, Dunford, Lindstrom)
- [ ] Is the page positioned against a clear competitor?
- [ ] Does it have a "for" statement? (April Dunford)
- [ ] Is it differentiated from generic category?
- [ ] Does it avoid "three is a crowd" (Al Ries)?

#### 2. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY (Muhammad, Hormozi, Zatta, Hoffmann)
- [ ] Is price anchored against expensive alternatives?
- [ ] Is value clearly communicated vs. cost?
- [ ] Are psychological pricing principles applied? ($19.99 vs $19)
- [ ] Is risk reversal prominent?

#### 3. SALES PSYCHOLOGY (Cardone, Tracy, Konrath, Gitomer)
- [ ] Are objections preemptively addressed?
- [ ] Is urgency/scarcity present and credible?
- [ ] Is social proof specific and compelling?
- [ ] Are CTAs action-oriented and outcome-focused?

#### 4. STORYTELLING (Godin, Miller, Vaynerchuk)
- [ ] Does it follow StoryBrand framework? (Customer as hero)
- [ ] Is it remarkable (Purple Cow)?
- [ ] Does it capture attention quickly?
- [ ] Is narrative clear and compelling?

#### 5. VALUE COMMUNICATION (Hormozi, Konrath, Baer)
- [ ] Are benefits quantified (not just features)?
- [ ] Is ROI clearly stated?
- [ ] Are outcomes specific and measurable?
- [ ] Is value stacked effectively?

#### 6. PERSUASION PRINCIPLES (Cialdini, Schwartz, Hopkins)
- [ ] Does it use reciprocity? (Free tier, free bookings)
- [ ] Is social proof specific? (Not generic)
- [ ] Is scarcity credible? (Not fake)
- [ ] Is authority established? (Expertise, credentials)

#### 7. CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION (Patel, Wiebe, Sugarman)
- [ ] Are headlines benefit-focused?
- [ ] Is copy scannable and clear?
- [ ] Are CTAs prominent and specific?
- [ ] Is visual hierarchy optimized?

## CONTENT REVIEW CHECKLIST

Before finalizing any landing page (except legal/compliance/privacy/terms):

### HERO SECTION
- [ ] Positions customer as hero (StoryBrand)
- [ ] States problem before solution
- [ ] Includes specific, quantified outcome
- [ ] Has clear, action-oriented CTA
- [ ] Uses emotional triggers (Lindstrom)

### PRICING SECTION
- [ ] Anchors against expensive alternatives (Muhammad)
- [ ] Shows value vs. cost clearly (Hormozi)
- [ ] Includes risk reversal (Hormozi)
- [ ] Uses psychological pricing principles (Muhammad)
- [ ] Highlights "most popular" option (Zatta)

### VALUE PROPOSITION
- [ ] Quantified benefits (not just features)
- [ ] Specific ROI/outcomes stated
- [ ] Competitive differentiation clear (Ries, Dunford)
- [ ] Emotional + rational appeal (Lindstrom)

### SOCIAL PROOF
- [ ] Specific numbers (not generic)
- [ ] Named sources (if available)
- [ ] Trust badges/certifications
- [ ] Peer validation (local/industry)

### URGENCY & SCARCITY
- [ ] Time-based urgency (Cardone)
- [ ] Quantity-based scarcity (if applicable)
- [ ] Loss aversion messaging (Muhammad)
- [ ] Credible and specific (not fake)

### OBJECTION HANDLING
- [ ] Preemptively addresses common objections (Tracy)
- [ ] Risk reversal prominent (Hormozi)
- [ ] Guarantees/assurances clear
- [ ] FAQ addresses key concerns

### CALL-TO-ACTION
- [ ] Outcome-focused (not generic "Sign Up")
- [ ] Specific and action-oriented (Gitomer)
- [ ] Prominent and high-contrast (Vaynerchuk)
- [ ] Includes risk reversal (Hormozi)

### STORYTELLING
- [ ] Follows StoryBrand structure (Miller)
- [ ] Customer is the hero
- [ ] Problem → Guide → Plan → Success/Failure
- [ ] Remarkable/unique angle (Godin)
