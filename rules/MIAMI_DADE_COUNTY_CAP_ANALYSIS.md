# Miami-Dade County: 33% Cap Analysis - Practice Area vs Combined Approach

## Executive Summary
This document analyzes Miami-Dade County's law firm capacity using two approaches:
1. **Practice-Area-Specific Caps** (each practice area has its own 33% cap)
2. **Combined Cap** (all practice areas share one 33% cap)

## Step 2: APPROACH A - Practice-Area-Specific Caps

### Each Practice Area Gets Its Own 33% Cap + 21% Premium Pre-Hold

| Practice Area | Total Firms | 33% Cap | Premium (21%) | Standard | Premium % | Standard % |
|--------------|-------------|---------|---------------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Personal Injury** | 1,300 | **429** | 90 | 339 | 21% | 79% |
| **Family Law** | 1,040 | **343** | 72 | 271 | 21% | 79% |
| **Immigration** | 780 | **257** | 54 | 203 | 21% | 79% |
| **Criminal Defense** | 624 | **206** | 43 | 163 | 21% | 79% |
| **Employment Law** | 416 | **137** | 29 | 108 | 21% | 79% |
| **Real Estate** | 364 | **120** | 25 | 95 | 21% | 79% |
| **Bankruptcy** | 260 | **86** | 18 | 68 | 21% | 79% |
| **Estate Planning** | 208 | **69** | 14 | 55 | 21% | 79% |
| **Workers' Comp** | 208 | **69** | 14 | 55 | 21% | 79% |
| **TOTAL** | **5,200** | **1,716** | **360** | **1,356** | **21%** | **79%** |

### Key Characteristics:
- ✅ **Fair Distribution:** Each practice area gets proportional seats
- ✅ **Prevents Dominance:** PI firms can't monopolize all seats
- ✅ **Practice-Area Protection:** Each area maintains its own competitive advantage
- ✅ **Clear Availability:** Users see availability for their specific practice area
- ⚠️ **More Complex:** Need to track availability per practice area
- ⚠️ **Less Flexible:** A PI firm can't use a Family Law seat if PI is full

## Step 3: APPROACH B - Combined Cap (Current Implementation)

### All Practice Areas Share One 33% Cap + 21% Premium Pre-Hold

| Metric | Calculation | Result |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Total Eligible Firms** | All practice areas combined | **5,200** |
| **33% Cap** | 5,200 × 0.33 | **1,716 seats** |
| **Premium Tier (21% pre-held)** | 1,716 × 0.21 | **360 seats** |
| **Standard Seats** | 1,716 - 360 | **1,356 seats** |

### Distribution (First-Come, First-Served):
- **No practice area guarantees** - seats go to whoever applies first
- **Potential imbalance:** If 1,000 PI firms apply first, they could take most seats
- **Flexibility:** Any firm can use any seat regardless of practice area

### Key Characteristics:
- ✅ **Simpler Implementation:** One pool to track
- ✅ **More Flexible:** Any firm can use any seat
- ✅ **First-Come, First-Served:** Fair in terms of timing
- ⚠️ **Potential Imbalance:** One practice area could dominate
- ⚠️ **Less Protection:** Practice areas with slower adoption lose out

## Step 4: Side-by-Side Comparison

### Scenario: High Demand from Personal Injury Firms

**APPROACH A (Practice-Area-Specific):**
- PI firms can only take up to **429 seats** (33% of 1,300 PI firms)
- Family Law firms still have **343 seats** available
- Each practice area maintains its competitive advantage
- **Result:** Balanced distribution

**APPROACH B (Combined):**
- PI firms apply first and take **1,000 seats**
- Family Law firms apply later and find only **716 seats** remaining
- PI firms get 77% of seats (1,000/1,300 = 77% penetration)
- Family Law gets 69% of seats (716/1,040 = 69% penetration)
- **Result:** Imbalanced, PI-dominated

## Step 8: Final Recommendation

### **RECOMMENDED: Practice-Area-Specific Caps**

**Reasoning:**
1. **Aligns with Core Value Proposition:** "If everyone has TrueVow, no one has an advantage" - this applies per practice area, not overall
2. **Fairer Distribution:** Prevents one practice area from dominating
3. **Better Competitive Protection:** Each practice area maintains its own advantage
4. **More Transparent:** Users see availability for their specific practice area
5. **Matches User Mental Model:** Attorneys think in terms of their practice area competition

**Trade-off:**
- More complex to implement, but provides better long-term value and fairness

## Step 14: Final Recommendation

### **RECOMMENDED: Practice-Area-Specific Caps**

**Primary Reasons:**
1. **Aligns with Core Logic:** "If everyone in your practice area has TrueVow, you have no advantage" - this is practice-area-specific, not overall
2. **Fairer Distribution:** Prevents one practice area from monopolizing seats
3. **Better Competitive Protection:** Each practice area maintains independent advantage
4. **More Transparent:** Users see availability for their specific competitive market
5. **Matches Attorney Mental Model:** Attorneys compete within their practice area, not across all areas

**Implementation Priority:**
- **Phase 1:** Update data structure to track practice areas
- **Phase 2:** Modify availability display to show practice-area-specific availability
- **Phase 3:** Update application form to capture practice area
- **Phase 4:** Update database to track enrollment per practice area

**Alternative (If Too Complex):**
- Keep combined approach but add clarification: "33% cap applies across all practice areas combined. Seats are first-come, first-served."

---

## Conclusion

Both approaches yield the same total seats (1,716), but **practice-area-specific caps provide better fairness, competitive protection, and user clarity** at the cost of increased implementation complexity.

**Recommendation:** Implement practice-area-specific caps for long-term value and fairness.
