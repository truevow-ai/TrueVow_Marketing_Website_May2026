### **#30 - Dual Arbitration Threshold (ICC vs. AAA)**

**What it is:**  
Two-tier arbitration system based on claim amount.

**Language to add:**

**Section 13.2:**
> * For claims **greater than USD 50,000**, arbitration shall be administered by the **ICC International Court of Arbitration** with the **seat of arbitration in Zurich, Switzerland**.
> * For claims **less than or equal to USD 50,000** (if Customer is U.S.-based), arbitration shall be conducted under the **AAA Commercial Rules**.

**Where to add:** Section 13 (Dispute Resolution) - replace single ICC rule

**Why valuable:**  
- Cost-effective for small claims
- U.S. attorneys more familiar with AAA
- Maintains Swiss seat for large claims
- Industry best practice (matches JAMS/AAA hybrids)

### **#32 - Founding Member Pricing Lock**

**What it is:**  
Detailed framework for promotional pricing with activity gates.

**Key provisions:**

**Definition:**
> "To retain promotional pricing (e.g., $25 per Qualified Booking) or free bookings, Customer must satisfy one of the defined **activity gates** each month."

**Penalty for Non-Compliance:**
> "**Failure for 3 consecutive months** shall result in the **permanent loss** of promotional benefits after a grace period; **status cannot be reinstated.**"

**Activity Gates (at least ONE must be met monthly):**
1. Generate ≥1 paid booking
2. Submit ≥1 settlement data point to SETTLE
3. Generate ≥1 qualified attorney referral (quarterly)

**Where to add:**  
- New Section 7.2 (Founding Member Activity Gate and Rate Lock)
- New Schedule B (Order Form) showing specific locked rates

**Why valuable:**  
- Prevents "churning" for promotional rates
- Incentivizes platform engagement
- Clear loss mechanism prevents disputes
- Permanent loss = no gaming the system
