I'm not a lawyer. I'm a small business owner who's built tech for Fortune 500 companies.

But I've always been close to the legal world — through clients, partners, friends who are solo attorneys.

And what I saw broke my heart.

The Breaking Point

Solo attorneys answering calls at 2 a.m. Missing their daughter's recitals. Settling for $27,500 when the case is worth $85,000 — not because they weren't great lawyers, but because they didn't have the data the insurance adjuster had.

I watched this pattern repeat for years. 70-hour weeks. Calls at 11 PM. Missing family dinners. Settling low because they were guessing and the adjusters were reading from Colossus.

They weren't losing because they were bad attorneys.

They were losing because the system is rigged.

Insurance companies have a $50 billion head start.

Solo attorneys have grit and a cell phone that rings at 2 AM.

So I Built Intakely (And Failed)

My first attempt was Intakely.ai — an AI-powered intake tool with "Benjamin" as your voice assistant.

I was chasing the AI hype. It was fast. It was smart. It booked consultations automatically.

Attorneys hated it.

Not because it didn't work. Because they didn't trust it.

  • "I can't risk recording client calls."
  • "AI hallucinated and told a client the wrong statute deadline."
  • "I don't trust AI to handle privileged information."
  • "It started giving fee estimates and legal advice — even with guardrails. That's unauthorized practice of law."
  • "What happens when the Bar audits me?"

They were right.

AI is a black box. You can't prove what it did or didn't store. You can't audit it. You can't defend it to the Bar.

So I did something radical: I shut down Intakely. Cut AI entirely. Rebuilt from scratch.

What emerged is TrueVow: A deterministic, rule-based system (like following a statute word-for-word, not a judge interpreting it) that vows zero AI, zero PHI storage, zero compromises.

Just a quiet partner who follows your rules.

Let Me Show You the Real Problem

Picture this: You're a solo PI attorney. You just spent 18 months fighting State Farm on a rear-end collision case. Clear liability. $47K in medical bills. Permanent back injury.

The adjuster offers $27,500.

You know it's low. Your gut says $70K minimum. But you don't have proof. You counter at $65K. They come back with $32K. You settle at $38,500.

Your client is thrilled. You just changed their life.

But you'll never know that 11 weeks ago, State Farm paid $81,000 for an identical case — same injury, same medical bills, same county — because that attorney had access to Colossus.

They're not smarter than you.

They just have a database you don't.

$50 Billion Head Start

Insurance companies have spent 40 years building Colossus — 11 million settlement outcomes, indexed by injury type, medical bills, jurisdiction, liability flags, and pre-existing conditions.

When an adjuster opens your file, they see:

  • "Rear-end, herniated disc, $47K medicals, Miami-Dade → Settlement range: $52K-$89K"
  • "Offer: $27,500 (low anchor)"
  • "Max authority: $78,000 (don't exceed unless liability weakens)"

You walk in with a demand letter and hope.

They walk in with 40 years of your colleagues' settlements.

Why Doesn't Someone Build One for Attorneys?

Great question. Here's why no one has:

1. Chicken-and-egg problem: You need 100,000+ settlements to be useful. But attorneys won't contribute until it's useful.

2. Privacy nightmare: Client names, case details, opposing counsel = privileged. One breach = malpractice lawsuits + Bar sanctions.

3. No one trusts competitors: "Why would I share my settlements with the firm across the street?"

4. VC-funded = data gets sold: Every legal tech startup backed by VCs eventually monetizes your data. Always.

So I built it differently.

How TrueVow Solves the Impossible

Problem 1: Chicken-and-egg

Solution: Build Benjamin (Intake) first. Give it to attorneys for $29/booking. Make it so good they can't leave. Then introduce Settle as a FREE bonus.

Now attorneys have a reason to contribute settlements: They're already TrueVow customers getting value every day.

Problem 2: Privacy nightmare

Solution: Zero-knowledge architecture. Settlements are 100% anonymous:

  • No client names
  • No attorney names
  • No case numbers
  • No opposing counsel
  • Only: Injury type, medical bills, jurisdiction, settlement amount, date

Blockchain-backed. Auditable. Bar-compliant. Unhackable.

Problem 3: Trust

Solution: Founding Members are co-builders, not competitors. You contribute settlements, you earn prediction credits. Everyone wins together.

It's not "share your secrets." It's "let's beat insurance companies together."

Problem 4: VC-funded data monetization

Solution: We're NOT VC-funded. We're bootstrapped. Founder-owned. No board pressuring us to "monetize user data" or "explore enterprise partnerships with Allstate."

Your data is not for sale. Our business model is usage-based ($29/booking), not data monetization. We make money when you win cases, not when we sell your information.
