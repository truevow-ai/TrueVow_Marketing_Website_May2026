📢 Change Notification & Operational Flexibility

⚠️ IMPORTANT: This list is indicative and non-exhaustive. TrueVow maintains operational flexibility to engage unlisted vendors for operational, emergency, or pre-authorized category purposes without violating the Master Services Agreement.

30-Day Notice (High-Risk Vendors Only): TrueVow will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide 30 days' written notice before adding new sub-processors that: (i) process call audio or transcripts; (ii) process customer PII beyond ephemeral routing; (iii) store customer data >24 hours; (iv) have access to unencrypted customer data.

Pre-Authorized Categories (No Notice Required): TrueVow may engage additional vendors in these categories without prior notice: Development tools, Infrastructure management, Monitoring and logging, Internal operations, Network security. See Section 6.6(b) of the MSA for details.

Customer Objection Right: Customers may object to new high-risk sub-processors within 15 days of notice. If TrueVow cannot accommodate the objection, Customer's exclusive remedy is to terminate the Agreement upon 30 days' written notice.

No Breach for Unlisted Vendors: Failure to list a vendor does NOT constitute a breach of the MSA and does NOT create any liability. TrueVow will use commercially reasonable efforts to keep the list current, but operational flexibility is preserved.

Email notifications sent to: Primary contact email address provided in Order Form.

📜 Governance & Compliance

Vendor Pass-Through Terms: All sub-processors (listed or unlisted) are governed by their respective terms of service, privacy policies, data processing agreements, and service level agreements, which are incorporated by reference into the TrueVow Master Services Agreement v3.0.

TrueVow's Obligations:

  • Maintain this list as indicative and non-exhaustive
  • Use commercially reasonable efforts to keep the list current
  • Provide 30-day notice for high-risk vendors (best efforts)
  • Ensure all sub-processors meet security standards (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, or equivalent)

⚠️ TrueVow's Liability Limitations:

  • TRUEVOW SHALL NOT BE LIABLE for any act, omission, error, breach, data loss, security incident, outage, or failure of any Sub-Processor (listed or unlisted)
  • Customer's exclusive remedy for Sub-Processor failures lies directly against the Sub-Processor under their own terms and limitations of liability
  • TrueVow does not control the Sub-Processors' systems, security practices, or operations
  • TrueVow is a customer of the Sub-Processors, not their owner, controller, or parent company

Customer's Rights:

  • Object to new high-risk sub-processors (15-day window)
  • Terminate the Agreement if objection cannot be accommodated (exclusive remedy)
  • Request DPAs directly from sub-processors (where applicable)
  • Audit sub-processor compliance documentation (upon reasonable notice)

Operational Flexibility: TrueVow may engage unlisted vendors for: (i) emergency purposes (failover, incident response, disaster recovery); (ii) operational purposes within pre-authorized categories; (iii) vendors that do NOT process customer data. Using an unlisted vendor does NOT violate the MSA and does NOT constitute a breach.
