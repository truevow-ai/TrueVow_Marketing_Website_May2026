TrueVow Sub-Processors

Material third-party service providers and vendor categories used to support TrueVow Services

Last Updated: July 1, 2026 | Schedule A Reference: Master Services Agreement v4.0

📋 About This List

This page identifies material third-party service providers and vendor categories that TrueVow currently uses or may use to support delivery, security, analytics, communications, billing, infrastructure, and operations for the Services. The specific providers used for a Customer may vary depending on product configuration, enabled features, tenant settings, integrations, failover routing, and deployment environment.

TrueVow uses commercially reasonable efforts to keep this list current. New high-risk sub-processors that materially process Customer Data are handled according to the notice and objection process described in the applicable Master Services Agreement.

Some tools listed below are operational vendors or self-hosted open-source components rather than external hosted sub-processors. Where a tool is operated entirely within TrueVow-controlled infrastructure and does not receive Customer Data as an external service provider, it is identified separately.

Pre-Authorized Categories (No Notice Required): TrueVow may engage additional vendors in these categories without prior notice: Development tools, Infrastructure management, Monitoring and logging, Internal operations, Network security. See Section 6.6(b) of the MSA for details.

⚠️ Disclaimer: All vendors listed below are used to support delivery of the Services. Data handling for these vendors is governed by vendor terms, security controls, and TrueVow's configuration. Provider use depends on product configuration, enabled features, tenant settings, integrations, failover routing, and deployment environment.

📢 Change Notice & Objection Process

Updates: TrueVow will use commercially reasonable efforts to keep this list current. Operational vendors within pre-authorized categories may be added as described in the MSA.

30-Day Notice (High-Risk Vendors Only): TrueVow will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide 30 days' written notice before adding new sub-processors that: (i) process call audio or transcripts; (ii) process customer PII beyond ephemeral routing; (iii) store customer data >24 hours; (iv) have access to unencrypted customer data.

Pre-Authorized Categories (No Notice Required): TrueVow may engage additional vendors in these categories without prior notice: Development tools, Infrastructure management, Monitoring and logging, Internal operations, Network security. See Section 6.6(b) of the MSA for details.

Customer Objection Right: Customers may object to new high-risk sub-processors within 15 days of notice. If TrueVow cannot accommodate the objection, Customer may terminate the Agreement upon 30 days' written notice.

Email notifications sent to: Primary contact email address provided in Order Form.

📜 Governance & Compliance

Vendor Pass-Through Terms: All sub-processors are governed by their respective terms of service, privacy policies, data processing agreements, and service level agreements, which are incorporated by reference into the TrueVow Master Services Agreement v4.0.

TrueVow's Obligations:

  • Maintain this list and use commercially reasonable efforts to keep it current
  • Provide 30-day notice for high-risk vendors (best efforts)
  • Review vendors using commercially reasonable security and operational criteria appropriate to the vendor's role, data access, and processing risk

Vendor Responsibility: Sub-processors operate under their own terms, privacy policies, service commitments, and data processing terms. TrueVow's responsibility for sub-processor acts and omissions is governed by the applicable agreement between TrueVow and the Customer.

Customer's Rights:

  • Object to new high-risk sub-processors (15-day window)
  • Terminate the Agreement if objection cannot be accommodated
  • Request DPAs directly from sub-processors (where applicable)
  • Audit sub-processor compliance documentation (upon reasonable notice)

Operational Flexibility: TrueVow may engage unlisted vendors for: (i) emergency purposes (failover, incident response, disaster recovery); (ii) operational purposes within pre-authorized categories; (iii) vendors that do not process customer data.
